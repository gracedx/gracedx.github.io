why

Prison Math Project pairs math mentors with mentees to write about math. My pen pal is Chris, who was fortunately also interested in learning linear algebra, as it was my topic of interest as well. I thus developed these letters as a way to learn proof-based linear algebra.

While the links here are to the letters, I am hoping to eventually adapt the letters and create more material to aid in studying proof-based linear algebra, a process that will refresh my own learnings as well.

The ultimate goal is to provide a guide to proof-based linear algebra accessible to any level, similar to Linear Algebra Done Right (Axler) but more simplified (e.g. explaining all the math notations and abbreviations).


what

there are only 3 note docs now, so there is significantly more material I am hoping to create. beloww I list topics covered in the existing materials:

  • proof-writing foundation: definitions, types of proofs, math symbols
  • basics of proof-based linear algebra: field of scalars, operations, examples
  • vector spaces: coordinates, addition, scalar multiplication, identities, inverses

how

I LaTeXed these by hand (i.e., no AI) in Overleaf. For the content, I referenced Linear Algebra Done Right (Axler) and most of all my Honors Linear Algebra notes taken under Prof. Christos Mantoulidis, who inspired me to major in math.

reflections

I hope to spend more time more consistently writing these to my pen pal as well as develop more of these materials.
