why

This app was created for a16z Boston Tech Week's Multi-Agent Orchestration Hackathon, cohosted by SundAI, AGI House, Weights & Biases, E14, and TNT. The backend was developed by Binam, Robin, Mike, and Noelia, while I focused on frontend, and Siddarth bootstrapped the startup script. The idea was developed by Binam, who works with multiple agents separately at work, but each agent operates without the context of the others. The goal was to create a "Zoom call" with multiple agents, so each agent has comprehensive context and they can collaborate on tasks together. Essentially, allowing agents to touch base, ensure alignment, identify synergies, and all the reasons there are meetings at work :p

For example, you may develop an app employing 2 agents, a software developer agent and a designer agent. Each agent can work on their tasks, but can interface as needed. If the designer decides on a design change that affects backend, it can communicate needs accordingly. To further reduce friction, this app allows for voice chat as well, where the user can speak to the agents and the agents can speak back.


what

overall features:
  • linking: linking of files for context
  • speech-to-text & text-to-speech: communicate via text or voice with agents
  • agent creation: add new agents, with designated roles
  • model selection: pi's agent harness offers access to hundreds of models, including from Anthropic and OpenAI
  • team chat: summaries of all agent responses & chats are collated in team chat sidebar
  • relay toggle: all summaries are relayed to all agents for context, but this toggle can also be turned off

how

For the UI, I heavily relied on Github Copilot in VScode for the workflow here, and most of all on my awesome team members. This was a super cool experience on wireframing and owning UI design, while being able to learn a ton and get a glimpse into the coding of my team members. Honestly, huge shoutouts to Binam and Robin, who I literally just met that day, and yet they took the time to answer every question I had and show me how to set up various AI coding tools. In terms of the real "how" for this app - ask the software engineers on the team :)
