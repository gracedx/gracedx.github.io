Payments Taxonomy: Tokens vs. Accounts

tldr: the rapid innovation of digital payments has led to new definitions and expansion of semantic understandings; this new framework consolidates both modern and historical perspectives toward tokens vs. accounts definitions

impact: incorporated into fintech onboarding repository at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston; presented to Chief Innovation Officer

why

At an internship at the Digital Payments & Research team at the Federal Reserve, I learned an incredible amount about the digital payments space, and kept coming across 2 terms: tokens and accounts.

With how relatively nascent the digital payments and blockchain technology space is compared to legacy rails, the terms tokens and accounts have vastly evolved in their definitions. This was something I experienced when trying to learn these concepts: I'd learn that the Bitcoin ecosystem uses tokens, while the Ethereum ecosystem uses accounts. Then I'd see some website talk about ETH tokens. I'd learn that cash is a token, but that tokenization of assets relates to turning assets digital (which cash certainly isn't). So on and so forth.

Further - a thorough analysis or synthesis of tokens and accounts did not yet exist. There were definitions, but these definitions were often incomprehensive, or more biased toward historical definitions rather than current usages of the terms in the ecosystems. Thus, especially as I had access to some of the top payments experts, I sought to develop a comprehensive framework.


what

problem:
  • Usage of the terms “tokens,” “accounts,” “push,” and “pull” are unstandardized and often conflated
  • Tokens are often related to "push" payments systems and accounts are often related to "pull" payments systems, but this relationship is not one-to-one
  • There exists a gap in the literature which synthesizes the nuanced definitions
process:
  • We break down terminology usage with a taxonomic approach of payments properties, systems, and intermediation
abstract:
  • In research and discussion of digital payments, semantic usage should be rigorously defined, with the following contexts considered:
    • data format ("UXTO" vs "account")
    • verification structure ("store of value" vs. "ledger")
    • initiator of transaction process
    • custody/relationship to value("push" vs. "pull")
    • verification structure("store of value" vs. "ledger")
    • relationship to value, permission to transact ("individual" vs. "third-party")
  • Especially with rise of AI and "tokens" taking on its n-th meaning, the above taxonomy enahances shared understanding and minimizes confusion due to vague terminology. Context specification plays a crucial role in clear definitions
