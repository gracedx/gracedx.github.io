type: web app full-stack landing page
status: developed june 2026
tools/stack: typescript, react.js, next.js, supabase


why

This app was hacked in 6 hours during SundAI Hack #127, Application Architecture, with Kerline, Rahul, and Aurora. AnkiConvo was the brainchild of our team lead Kerline, who sought to develop an app where you could practice Anki by voice on the go. Kerline and Rahul developed the AnkiConvo app, including AWS integration to ensure that AnkiConvo could be used on the go on your phone, while Aurora and I developed the user landing page, where users would sign up and pay for the app.


what

landing page features:
  • user sign up: authentication via supabase
  • light/dark toggle: viewers can toggle between light & dark mode
  • faqs: anki-style card flip faqs
  • payments: pricing model

how

This landing page was heavily Claude coded from start to finish, with me stepping in to troubleshoot any connections/interfacing across Vercel and Supabase. We had also inteneded to integrate Stripe (for payments) and Resend (for email API outreach) but due to time constraints had to delete those portions prior to the demo.

reflections

This was an incredibly fun project and team to work with! I'm hoping to take some free time to come back to this and get the Stripe and Resend integrations working, but overall it was a really fun experience, and super grateful to my teammates for taking the time to teach and also just have fun. The demos from the other hackathon participants were also amazing as always; I think one of my favorites was a Boston parking finder, a Boston events feed, and a voice agent time tracker.
