flash loans

tldr: an analysis of flash loan use cases and externalities for the crypto market

impact: address gap in literature on comprehensive flash loan use cases and externalities



presentation (22 slides) →
paper (16 pages) →

type: research
status: completed april 2024


why

This paper was written in partnership with my classmate Adi Zytek for our economics capstone. I'd been hoping to research about flash loans, a technology I'd just heard offhandedly about during a past internship but which fascinated me; I knew Adi had done research under a econ professor already and our skills sets could complement well.

The simple way to explain a flash loan is like an instaneous, low-/no-interest, low-/no-risk loan using smart contracts/blockchain technology.

Especially as flash loans are such a relatively phenomenon, we sought to 1) survey existing use cases, a gap in the existing literature given the newness of flash loans, and 2) analyze externalities from flash loans on the crypto market.


what

definition:
  • type of uncollateralized debt in which debt (plus fees) must be repaid within the same block as the initial loan
    • if not repaid, entire loan transaction is canceled, making it a no-/low-risk loan for loaner
use cases:
  • arbitrage: most common use case of flash loans is to facilitate arbitrage of cryptocurrencies
    • if a token’s price denominated in another token is cheaper on another exchange, investors can use a flash loan to purchase the token on the cheaper exchange, sell it on the more expensive one, and then pay back the original loan
  • self-liquidation: using the flash loan to essentially pay back another tokenized collateralized loan, by borrowing the same type of token that has been previously borrowed to repay that loan\
    • this is often used to ensure sufficient collateratalization, or alternatively to facilitate arbitrage

  • collateral swapping: as the name suggests, flash loans can be used to swap collateral. if a user has using token A as collateral for B, but would rather use token C as collateral, they can taken out a flash loan for token A

benefits to the market:
  • increased efficiency from arbitrage
  • increased liquidity
  • lower barriers to entry
risks to the market:
  • reduced friction for malicious players
  • governance relies on whales' behavior, which creates higher risks and volatility
  • free flow of capital facilitates price manipulation
risk mitigation strategies:
  • force critical transactions to span 2 blocks
  • Merkle proof checks
  • interest pricing to create friction
  • governance timelocks to mitigate governance-/voting-based attacks
  • Weighted-average price oracles to mitigate price manipulation

how

This research surveyed existing research on flash loans, existing research on traditional loans, and alternative sources such as blogs and twitter posts on flash loan use cases and processes, given the limited academic literature on the topic. We then analyzed overall positive and negative externalities based on the synthesis of literature There was also an analysis of traditional methods of pricing interest (Edelberg 2006) but which varied significantly from flash loan interest pricing given the zero risk to lender with blockchain technology.

reflections

Flash loans sound like magic: you only receive the loan if you paid it back.

With that said, flash loans, as they are, are fairly limited in scope to the cryptocurrency world and thus have a relatively low impact on the layperson, as most people consider themselves quite removed from blockchain technology. Even within the payments and fintech world, not all have heard of flash loans. While this research primarily analyzed existing use cases, if I ever revisit this, I'd love to take the time to be more future-looking: ideate potential high-impact use cases, and model the practicalities and possibilities of flash loans at scale.
